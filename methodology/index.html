Methodology

Be explicit about what the data can support.

PharmaPath uses openFDA because it can expose real shortage, listing, manufacturer, approval, and recall context. It cannot answer whether a specific pharmacy has stock right now, so the product does not pretend otherwise.

Checking current data-source status…

What PharmaPath reads directly from openFDA
  • NDC and product listing records
  • FDA drug shortage and discontinuation entries
  • Drugs@FDA approval and submission metadata
  • FDA enforcement and recall notices
What the access signal is actually doing

PharmaPath combines shortage entries, discontinuation notes, manufacturer concentration, active listing breadth, and recent recall context to label the search as steadier, mixed, or higher friction. That label is always presented as a signal-based estimate.

What the product will not claim
  • That a specific store has the drug on the shelf
  • That the patient will definitely be able to fill it today
  • That insurance will cover the medication or a substitute
  • That a shortage entry automatically means the drug is unavailable everywhere
Why patient and prescriber pages are separate

Patients need a short explanation, likely friction signal, and next questions. Prescribers need the evidence trail: shortage status, manufacturer breadth, formulation spread, recall context, and whether alternative planning is warranted.
