How PharmaPath reads FDA data

Known facts first, inferences second, missing data stated plainly.

01

Find the FDA-listed product family

We match the medication search to openFDA listing and approval records, then group the likely product families and formulations.

02

Pull shortage, discontinuation, and recall signals

We surface matching FDA shortage entries, discontinuation notes, manufacturer concentration, and recent recall context when it exists.

03

Translate that into careful guidance

The product labels its summary as an estimate. It tells users what looks risky, what looks steadier, and what openFDA simply cannot tell them.

Directly known

  • FDA product listings and matching NDCs
  • Manufacturer and sponsor names in the FDA records
  • Drug shortage entries and discontinuation notices
  • Recall notices and report dates

Inferred carefully

  • Whether access looks steadier, mixed, or higher friction
  • Whether limited manufacturers may tighten supply options
  • Whether formulation breadth gives more prescribing flexibility

Not available

  • Real-time shelf inventory at a local pharmacy
  • Insurance adjudication or patient copay
  • Wholesaler stock or store-to-store transfer success
  • Guaranteed same-day availability
